# Inventory-Management-Analysis
Inventory Management Analysis Dashboard, Power BI: Analyzed 303 SKUs worth ₹77.33M warehouse value using ABC classification, demand segmentation (Uniform/Variable/Uncertain), and ITR tracking. Identified 284 SKUs below reorder point, 89.77% below safety stock, with High-Value items under Uniform Demand turning 25.43x annually.

## Key Findings

- Critical Reorder Breach — 284 out of 303 SKUs (93.7%) have fallen below their reorder point, indicating a near-portfolio-wide replenishment emergency requiring immediate procurement prioritisation by value tier
- Safety Stock Depletion — 89.77% of SKUs classified as Below Safety Stock with only 19 SKUs (6.27%) maintaining healthy In Stock status, signalling that the demand buffer has been largely consumed across the catalogue
- Warehouse Capital Concentration — ₹77.33M locked in warehouse inventory against a turnover ratio of 5.42, sitting at the upper edge of the healthy 4–6x benchmark — masking significant SKU-level variation beneath the average
- Turnover Ratio Disparity — High-Value SKUs under Uniform Demand turn at 25.43x annually vs Less-Value SKUs under Uncertain Demand at just 1.38x, revealing a 18x performance gap between the most and least efficient inventory segments
- Revenue Concentration (Pareto Effect) — High-Value SKUs generate ₹0.30bn in annual revenue while Less-Value SKUs — nearly 200 products — contribute the smallest revenue share, confirming a classic 80/20 distribution and a portfolio rationalisation opportunity
- Working Capital Inefficiency — Medium-Value SKUs under Uncertain Demand hold ₹25.3M in current stock — the largest single inventory bucket by value — yet deliver disproportionately low turnover, representing a trapped working capital risk
- Demand Volatility Risk — Uncertain Demand category consistently underperforms across all value tiers in turnover ratio, with High-Value Uncertain at 5.62x vs - Uniform at 25.43x — indicating forecasting gaps that inflate safety stock requirements
- Rising Demand vs Falling Stock — Weekly sales trend shows an uptick approaching April 2020 precisely as stock levels are depleted — a convergence of increasing demand and insufficient inventory that defines the peak stockout risk window
- Less-Value SKU Drag — ~200 Less-Value SKUs with 1.38x turnover under Uncertain Demand are consuming warehouse space, insurance, and procurement bandwidth disproportionate to their revenue contribution — a candidate for discontinuation or make-to-order transition
- SKU-Level Visibility Gap — Only 2 SKUs (3264CA, 3271CA) maintain stock above both safety stock and reorder thresholds — confirming that healthy inventory positions are the exception, not the operational baseline
